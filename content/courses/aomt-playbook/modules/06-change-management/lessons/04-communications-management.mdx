Effective change communications ensure that each stakeholder group receives the right message, through the right channel, at the right time. Poor communications — even of genuinely good changes — generate resistance, confusion, and adoption failure.

<Callout type="concept" title="The 5W Communications Framework">
Every communication should answer: **Who** is the intended audience? **What** do they need to know? **When** do they need to know it? **Where** (which channel) will the message be delivered? **Why** does this change matter to them specifically?
</Callout>

## Stakeholder Message Differentiation

| Audience | Core Message | Channel | Timing |
|---|---|---|---|
| Executive Leadership | Financial impact, strategic alignment, PSC Rate Case risk mitigation | Executive briefing, board presentation | Before formal announcement |
| Middle Management | What changes in their team's workflow, what support is available, what they will be measured on | Manager briefing, Q&A session | 1–2 weeks before rollout |
| Frontline Staff | What changes in their daily work, how it makes their job easier, what happens if they struggle | Team meetings, job aids, supervisor conversations | At rollout, with ongoing reinforcement |
| IT/Security | Technical requirements, data governance, security review process | Technical working sessions | Early in solution design |
