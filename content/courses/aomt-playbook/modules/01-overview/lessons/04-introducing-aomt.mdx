## Two Core Principles (Retained)

<Callout type="concept" title="Data First">
Ingest and interrogate the data landscape to reveal and verify evidence-based opportunities. Instead of starting with stakeholder impressions, start with data patterns and validate them against stakeholder experience. For insurance organizations, this means confronting the reality of siloed policy, claims, and actuarial data before any AI deployment can succeed at scale.
</Callout>

<Callout type="concept" title="AI First">
Design solutions that take full advantage of AI capabilities to make quantum gains in efficiency. Ask "what becomes possible if AI handles this?" rather than "how can AI speed up step 3?" For insurance, this reframes underwriting, claims triage, and customer service from document-heavy human workflows to AI-augmented decision pipelines.
</Callout>

## The +AI to AI+ Maturity Progression

  { title: "AI+ Posture (Select Domains)", description: "AI becomes a core operating mechanism in domains where evidence and organizational readiness justify it. ]} />

<Callout type="tip" title="Insurance-Specific Starting Points">
For a low-maturity mid-size carrier, the highest-readiness +AI entry points are typically: **claims triage and routing** (structured data, clear outcomes, high repetition), **underwriting data preparation** (AI extracts and structures submission data, underwriter makes the decision), and **policy servicing inquiry handling** (AI drafts responses, agent reviews). Start where your data is cleanest and your process is most repetitive.
</Callout>

## Why Organizations Stall (and How Insurance Amplifies Each Risk)

| Failure Mode | General Pattern | Insurance Amplifier |
|---|---|---|
| Sequential thinking | Phase-by-phase delivery creates months of latency | Annual planning cycles and project-based budgeting reinforce waterfall behavior |
| Change management as afterthought | Resistance is entrenched by the time training begins | Underwriters and adjusters have high autonomy expectations; early engagement is non-negotiable |
| Perfect-before-deploy culture | Over-engineering delays feedback | Actuarial and compliance cultures reward thoroughness — explicitly manage the perfection-vs-iteration tension |
| AI disconnected from operations | Central AI team, no frontline involvement | Claims and underwriting are siloed; AI teams rarely have domain credibility without embedded SMEs |
| Data readiness under-invested | AI pilots fail to scale | Policy, claims, and billing data in separate legacy systems is the norm — treat data foundation as a prerequisite, not a parallel track |
