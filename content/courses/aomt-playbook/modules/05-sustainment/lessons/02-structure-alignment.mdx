The Structure pillar ensures that the organization is aligned to sustain the AI solution through clear roles, responsibilities, accountability mechanisms, and incentive structures. Without structural alignment, even well-trained Pods will encounter organizational resistance that forces regression to old behaviors under operational pressure.

<Callout type="concept" title="Structure Follows the Pod">
In a Pod-based operating model, structural alignment means ensuring that the organization around the Pod — leadership, HR, finance, compliance — supports rather than contradicts what the Pod is trying to accomplish. The most common structural failure is a Pod optimizing for an AI-driven outcome while the performance management system still measures and rewards the old behavior it's trying to replace.
</Callout>

## Key Structural Alignment Questions

- **Who owns this outcome?** Identify the Pod's Product Manager as the accountable outcome owner — not a committee.
- **Who funds it?** AI solutions need sustained investment beyond the initial deployment. Confirm budget allocation for ongoing model maintenance, data pipeline costs, and Pod continuity.
- **What happens when key Pod members leave?** Define the knowledge transfer process. Pod knowledge should live in documentation and code, not solely in people's heads.
- **Does the org chart enable or block the Pod?** If the Pod's domain SME reports to a function that is resistant to the change, the reporting structure may need to evolve alongside the solution.

## Incentive Alignment — Insurance Context

<Callout type="concept" title="Align Incentives with Desired Behaviors">
People respond to what they are measured on. If claims adjusters are measured on closure volume but the AI solution prioritizes accuracy and straight-through rate, the metrics create a conflict that the AI will lose. Ensure that performance metrics, bonus structures, and recognition programs are aligned with the outcomes the Pod is designed to deliver.
</Callout>

| Old Incentive | Behavior It Drives | New Incentive | Desired Behavior |
|---|---|---|---|
| Claims closure volume | Rush settlements, skip AI recommendations | Accuracy rate + customer satisfaction | Use AI triage; close right, not just fast |
| Underwriting throughput | Bypass AI data prep, use manual shortcuts | Submission-to-bind cycle time + loss ratio | Leverage AI data extraction to speed quality decisions |
| Call handle time | Give quick answers without AI assistance | First-contact resolution rate | Use AI draft responses; verify before sending |
| Individual performance only | Protect personal workflow, resist Pod changes | Pod outcome + individual contribution | Invest in shared Pod success |

<Callout type="warning" title="Misaligned Incentives Kill AI Solutions">
In insurance, this risk is amplified by strong professional identity among underwriters and adjusters. They have built careers on expertise and judgment. AI tools that feel like they are replacing (rather than augmenting) that judgment will face active resistance unless the incentive and recognition structures explicitly affirm that using AI well is a mark of professional excellence, not a crutch.
</Callout>
