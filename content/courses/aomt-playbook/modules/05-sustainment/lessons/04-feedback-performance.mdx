The Feedback pillar provides the monitoring systems, review cadences, and escalation paths that detect and correct drift before it compounds. Without feedback, structural alignment and learning support cannot self-correct — the system has no mechanism to know it is drifting.

<Callout type="concept" title="Monitoring Cadence Hierarchy">
Effective performance monitoring operates at four levels: daily operational metrics (flags same-day issues), weekly trend reviews (identifies emerging patterns), monthly performance reviews (assesses solution health), and quarterly strategic reviews (evaluates alignment with organizational goals). Each level has specific decision triggers and escalation paths.
</Callout>

## Key Performance Indicators

For each AI solution, define a small number of leading indicators (predict future performance) and lagging indicators (measure outcomes already achieved):

- **Leading Indicators:** Solution adoption rate, user confidence scores, data quality metrics, model accuracy on test cases
- **Lagging Indicators:** First-visit resolution rate, customer satisfaction scores, repeat dispatch rate, cost per service call

<BenefitsGraphic />

<Callout type="warning" title="Named Ownership for Every Metric">
Every KPI must have a named owner — a specific person responsible for monitoring it, interpreting it, and triggering action when thresholds are crossed. Metrics with shared ownership have no owner. When a metric deteriorates, "everyone" is responsible means no one is.
</Callout>
