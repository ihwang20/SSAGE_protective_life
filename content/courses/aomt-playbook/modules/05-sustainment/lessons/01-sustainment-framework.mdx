Deploying an AI solution is only the beginning. Without a deliberate sustainment framework, even the most effective solutions will degrade over time as processes drift, knowledge fades, and organizational attention shifts to the next priority.

## The Three Pillars

<SustainmentPillars />

<ProcessFlow steps={[
  { title: "Structure (Alignment)", description: "Clear roles, responsibilities, accountability mechanisms, and incentive structures that ensure the organization is aligned to sustain the AI solution permanently. Without structure, teams revert to old behaviors under operational pressure." },
  { title: "Support (Learning)", description: "Ongoing training, knowledge management, and coaching that maintain organizational competency as staff turns over, the solution evolves, and new capabilities emerge." },
  { title: "Feedback (Performance)", description: "Performance monitoring, escalation paths, and continuous improvement cycles that detect and correct drift before it compounds into failure." }
]} />

<Callout type="concept" title="All Three Pillars Are Required">
Structure without Support means people have clear roles but lack the knowledge to perform them. Support without Feedback means people are trained but have no way to know if their performance is meeting standards. Feedback without Structure means data is collected but no one is accountable for acting on it. All three pillars must be present and reinforcing for sustainment to succeed.
</Callout>

## The Drift Problem

**Drift** is the gradual degradation of solution effectiveness over time. It occurs when:

- **Process drift** — Users develop workarounds that bypass the intended workflow
- **Model drift** — AI model accuracy declines as underlying data patterns change
- **Priority drift** — Organizational attention shifts and sustainment activities are deprioritized
- **Knowledge drift** — Staff turnover dilutes institutional knowledge about how and why the solution works

<Callout type="warning" title="Drift Is Invisible Until It Compounds">
Drift rarely announces itself. A 1% weekly decline in process adherence compounds to a 40% decline over a year. By the time the degradation becomes visible in operational metrics, significant remediation effort is required. The sustainment framework's primary purpose is to detect and correct drift before it compounds.
</Callout>
