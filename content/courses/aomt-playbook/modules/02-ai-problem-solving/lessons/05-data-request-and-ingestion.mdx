A formal data request is the practitioner's mechanism for specifying what data is needed, why it is needed, at what granularity, and over what time period. A well-crafted data request accelerates data acquisition and reduces back-and-forth with data owners.

<Callout type="concept" title="Request Before You Need">
Data acquisition typically has a lag of 1–2 weeks. Submit your data request at the start of the engagement, not when you're ready to analyze. Request broadly — it is easier to ignore data you don't need than to wait for data that wasn't requested.
</Callout>

## Data Request Components

- **Dataset name and description** — What the data contains
- **Time period** — Minimum window needed (typically 12–18 months for trend analysis)
- **Granularity** — Transaction-level vs. summary; daily vs. weekly vs. monthly
- **Key fields** — Specific columns or variables needed
- **Preferred format** — CSV, Excel, database extract, API
- **Data owner** — Who to contact for questions or access
- **Analytic purpose** — Why this data is needed (helps data owners prioritize)
