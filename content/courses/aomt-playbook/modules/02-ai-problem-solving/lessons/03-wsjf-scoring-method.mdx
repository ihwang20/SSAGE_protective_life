**Weighted Shortest Job First (WSJF)** is a quantitative scoring framework borrowed from Agile and Lean portfolio management. It ranks opportunities by dividing their "Cost of Delay" by their estimated implementation effort, producing a defensible, rank-ordered backlog.

<Callout type="concept" title="WSJF Formula">
**WSJF Score = Cost of Delay ÷ Job Size**

Where Cost of Delay = Business Value + Time Criticality + Risk Reduction/Opportunity Enablement
</Callout>

## Scoring Dimensions

<WSJFScoringFactors />

| Dimension | What It Measures | Scale |
|---|---|---|
| **Business Value** | Financial and operational impact of implementing this opportunity | 1–10 (relative) |
| **Time Criticality** | Cost of delaying implementation — urgency based on external deadlines, competitive dynamics, or compounding risk | 1–10 (relative) |
| **Risk Reduction** | Value of reducing known risks or enabling future opportunities | 1–10 (relative) |
| **Job Size (Effort)** | Implementation complexity, timeline, and resource requirements | 1–10 (relative; higher = more effort) |

<Callout type="tip" title="Use Relative Scoring">
Score opportunities relative to each other, not against an absolute scale. The first opportunity scored becomes the anchor — all subsequent opportunities are scored relative to it. This produces a more consistent and defensible ranking than attempting to score against absolute criteria.
</Callout>
