This exercise develops your ability to evaluate the quality of AI prompts and their likely effectiveness in a diagnostic context. Understanding what makes a prompt effective is as important as knowing what question to ask.

<Callout type="concept" title="What Makes a Prompt Effective?">
An effective diagnostic prompt is **specific** (targets a precise question, not a broad topic), **grounded** (references actual data or context), **bounded** (limits the scope of the response), and **action-oriented** (requests analysis, not just description).
</Callout>

## Scoring Rubric

| Dimension | 1 – Weak | 3 – Adequate | 5 – Strong |
|---|---|---|---|
| Specificity | Vague, could apply to any problem | Relevant to the situation with some specifics | Targets a precise question with named variables |
| Context | No context provided | General situation described | Specific data, metrics, or findings referenced |
| Scope Bounding | Open-ended, no limits | Some limits implied | Explicit scope and format specified |
| Action Orientation | Asks AI to describe or explain | Asks AI to analyze or compare | Asks AI to identify, rank, or recommend with criteria |
