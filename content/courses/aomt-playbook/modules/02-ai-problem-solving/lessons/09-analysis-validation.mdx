AI analysis must be validated before informing recommendations. Validation confirms that the analysis is technically sound, contextually appropriate, and consistent with known facts about the situation.

<Callout type="warning" title="Never Rely on a Single AI Analysis">
AI models can produce plausible-sounding but incorrect analysis. Correlation can be spurious. Pattern recognition can surface noise as signal. Always validate AI findings through at least two of three methods: re-running with different prompts, comparing against a second tool, and qualitative validation with domain experts.
</Callout>

## Validation Methods

<ProcessFlow steps={[
  { title: "Prompt Variation Testing", description: "Re-run the same analytical question with different prompt structures, different levels of specificity, and different framing. Consistent findings across prompt variations increase confidence." },
  { title: "Cross-Tool Comparison", description: "Run the same analysis in a second AI tool. Major discrepancies indicate that the finding may be tool-specific rather than a genuine data pattern." },
  { title: "Domain Expert Confirmation", description: "Present findings to a domain expert (e.g., an experienced dispatcher, a senior technician) and ask whether the pattern makes operational sense. Domain experts often quickly identify whether a finding is real or an artifact." },
  { title: "Benchmark Comparison", description: "Compare findings against industry benchmarks or historical baselines. A finding that shows Metro Cable's FCR at 62% is more credible when external benchmarks confirm top-quartile performance is 85%." }
]} />
