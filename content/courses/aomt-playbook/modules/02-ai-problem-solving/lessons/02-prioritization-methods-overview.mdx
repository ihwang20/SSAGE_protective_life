With validated opportunities documented, Phase 2 begins: prioritization. The goal is to rank opportunities based on business value, time criticality, risk reduction, and implementation effort — then sequence them into an actionable roadmap.

The methodology provides two complementary prioritization methods. The right choice depends on the maturity of your opportunity documentation, the number of opportunities, and the audience.

<SectionDivider />

## Comparing the Two Methods

| Factor | WSJF (Weighted Shortest Job First) | Value vs. Complexity Matrix |
|---|---|---|
| **Complexity** | Higher — scoring 4 dimensions on a 1–10 scale | Lower — qualitative 2×2 grid placement |
| **Time Required** | 90–120 minutes for 5–7 opportunities | 45–60 minutes for the same set |
| **Best For** | Agile environments, large portfolios (10+ opportunities) | Quick prioritization, smaller sets (3–5) |
| **Output** | Rank-ordered list with numerical scores | Quadrant placement with action guidance |
| **Stakeholder Buy-In** | Strong — transparent, repeatable scoring | Strong — visual simplicity resonates with executives |

<Callout type="tip" title="Combine for Maximum Impact">
Start with the **Value vs. Complexity Matrix** in a stakeholder workshop to build consensus on quadrant placement. Then apply **WSJF scoring** to the Quick Wins and Strategic Bets quadrants for precise sequencing. This two-pass approach leverages the strengths of both methods: the Matrix builds alignment, WSJF builds the roadmap.
</Callout>

<Callout type="warning" title="Consistency Over Methodology">
The specific methodology matters less than **consistent application** and stakeholder buy-in. Pick one approach, apply it rigorously, and use the results to drive decisions. Switching methods mid-process undermines credibility.
</Callout>
