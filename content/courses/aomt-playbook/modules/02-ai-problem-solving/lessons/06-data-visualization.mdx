Effective data visualization is not decoration — it is analysis. The right visualization reveals patterns that tabular data obscures, enables rapid hypothesis testing, and communicates findings to stakeholders who cannot read raw data.

<Callout type="concept" title="Visualization Drives Insight">
The most important visualization is the one that changes your hypothesis. Start with exploratory visualizations to understand the data landscape, then build explanatory visualizations to communicate findings. These serve different audiences and different purposes.
</Callout>

## Visualization Types for Diagnostic Work

<VisualizationTypes />

| Chart Type | Best For | Common Use in Diagnosis |
|---|---|---|
| Bar Chart | Comparing categories | FCR rates by technician zone, job type, or certification level |
| Scatter Plot | Relationships between variables | Travel time vs. first-visit resolution rate correlation |
| Heat Map | Patterns across two dimensions | Resolution rate by zone and time of day |
| Trend Line | Change over time | FCR rate decline over 12 months |
| Distribution | Spread and outliers | Travel time distribution by zone |

<Callout type="tip" title="Speed Advantage">
A visualization that would take 2–4 hours to build in traditional BI tools can be generated in under 2 minutes through AI prompting. This speed enables practitioners to explore dozens of visual perspectives in a single analysis session, dramatically increasing the likelihood of discovering meaningful patterns.
</Callout>
