The Value vs. Complexity Matrix is a visual 2×2 grid that plots opportunities by their expected value against their implementation complexity. It is faster to execute than WSJF, requires less data, and produces a quadrant placement that stakeholders can absorb at a glance.

| Quadrant | Value | Complexity | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Quick Wins** | High | Low | Do first — maximum ROI for minimum effort |
| **Strategic Bets** | High | High | Plan carefully — high value justifies the investment |
| **Fill-Ins** | Low | Low | Do when capacity exists — low priority |
| **Avoid** | Low | High | Deprioritize — not worth the effort |

<Callout type="concept" title="Workshop Tool">
The Value vs. Complexity Matrix is particularly effective as a workshop facilitation tool. Print each opportunity on a card. Have stakeholders physically place the cards on the matrix grid. The act of physically moving cards generates discussion, surfaces disagreements, and builds shared understanding of trade-offs.
</Callout>
