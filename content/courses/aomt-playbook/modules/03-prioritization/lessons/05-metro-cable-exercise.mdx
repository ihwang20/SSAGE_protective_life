# Metro Cable Exercise: Prioritization

This exercise applies the full Opportunity Prioritization toolkit to the validated Metro Cable findings. You will move from raw diagnostic outputs through structured documentation, WSJF scoring, and Value vs. Complexity placement — the same sequence used to build the final roadmap recommendation.

<Callout type="concept" title="Where You Are in the Engagement">
The diagnostic phase is complete. Three root causes have been validated: technician-to-job skill mismatches in underperforming zones, systematic underestimation of complex job durations, and absence of real-time re-routing. You must now document, score, and sequence these opportunities before presenting a prioritized roadmap to the Metro Cable executive committee.
</Callout>

## Part 2: WSJF Scoring

<ReflectionPrompt
  question="Using the validated Metro Cable opportunity set — (1) AI Skill-Match Dispatch, (2) Dynamic Job Duration Calibration, and (3) Real-Time Re-routing — score each opportunity on the four WSJF components (Business Value, Time Criticality, Risk Reduction, Job Size) using the Fibonacci scale (1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 13). Calculate the WSJF score for each and explain the sequencing logic."
  answer="Suggested scoring:

AI Skill-Match Dispatch: Business Value = 13 (primary driver of the accuracy gap, largest projected impact), Time Criticality = 8 (customer churn is accelerating; delay compounds the loss), Risk Reduction = 5 (reduces operational variability and call-back costs), Job Size = 2 (existing data and API integrations; prototypable in weeks). Cost of Delay = 26. WSJF = 26 ÷ 2 = 13.0.

Dynamic Job Duration Calibration: Business Value = 8 (reduces scheduling compression; enables the dispatch engine to function reliably), Time Criticality = 5 (moderate urgency; calibration data is stable), Risk Reduction = 3 (lower risk surface than dispatch routing), Job Size = 1 (primarily a data update and algorithm adjustment; no new infrastructure). Cost of Delay = 16. WSJF = 16 ÷ 1 = 16.0.

Real-Time Re-routing: Business Value = 5 (addresses cascade failures; important but lower baseline impact than the other two), Time Criticality = 5 (moderate; current workaround is manual coordinator intervention), Risk Reduction = 3 (reduces afternoon schedule degradation), Job Size = 3 (requires real-time data feeds and standby pool management). Cost of Delay = 13. WSJF = 13 ÷ 3 = 4.3.

Prioritized sequence: Duration Calibration first (WSJF 16.0 — small effort, high leverage as a prerequisite for accurate dispatch modeling), then Skill-Match Dispatch (WSJF 13.0 — largest value, enables immediate FCR improvement), then Real-Time Re-routing (WSJF 4.3 — valuable but best implemented once the upstream scheduling inputs are correct)."
